Project: “Straining the great southern
Melting Pot” 

The aim of this project is to explore and analyze a dataset containing over 53000 reviews of
restaurants in Atlanta (Georgia), scraped from Google Maps in October 2023.
The project focuses on utilizing text mining techniques to answer two primary information requirements:
How well can we classify a restaurant’s cuisine type using the content of
their reviews as input? (Multilabel Classification) and How strong is the relationship
between the polarity of a comments and its score? (Sentiment Analysis). Additionally,
exploring any emergent relationships, such as clustering dishes mentioned in reviews and identifying
cuisine types based on these clusters will also be relevant.

The project is particularly important to the Atlanta Tourism Board, who hopes to use
the insights gained from this analysis to enhance their understanding of the city’s restaurant
landscape. The goal is to determine if “e-Word-of-Mouth” (textual comments from reviews)
can be a more effective method of classifying restaurant types and evaluating quality than the
predefined labels and star ratings used on platforms like Google Maps.
